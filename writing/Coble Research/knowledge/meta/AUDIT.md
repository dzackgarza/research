# Mathematical Audit — Coble-Paper

## 1. Wrong $\delta$-invariant for $S_{\mathrm{Co}}$

**Location**: `030_Cusp_Correspondence.tex` L9

**As written**: $S_\mathrm{Co} = (11, 11, 0)_1$

**Claim**: Should be $(11, 11, 1)_1$.

**Proof**: The lattice is $S_\mathrm{Co} = \langle -2 \rangle \oplus E_{10}(2)$.
For a 2-elementary lattice $L$, the invariant $\delta = 0$ iff $q_L(x) \in \mathbb{Z}$ for all $x$ in the discriminant group $A_L = L^\vee / L$, and $\delta = 1$ otherwise.
The summand $\langle -2 \rangle$ has discriminant group $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ generated by $e/2$, and $q(e/2) = -2/4 = -1/2 \notin \mathbb{Z}$.
Therefore $\delta = 1$.
This matches the value $(11, 11, 1)$ stated in `010_Lattices.tex` L13, `020_Moduli_Construction.tex` L61-62, and the mirror move tree itself at L16. The occurrence at L9 is a typo.

**Resolution**: Fixed in `sections/030_Cusp_Correspondence.tex` L9 by changing $(11, 11, 0)_1$ to $(11, 11, 1)_1$.

## 2. Genus-degree formula sign error

**Location**: `999_Appendix.tex` L14

**As written**: $$p_a(C) = \frac{1}{2}(d-1)(d-2) + \frac{1}{2}\sum_i m_i(m_i - 1)$$

**Claim**: The $+$ should be $-$.

**Proof**: The standard genus formula for plane curves with ordinary singularities (e.g. Hartshorne IV.1.8, or Griffiths-Harris p. 280) is: $$p_a(C) = \frac{(d-1)(d-2)}{2} - \sum_i \frac{m_i(m_i-1)}{2}$$

Each singularity *reduces* the arithmetic genus.
**Numerical check** with $d = 6$ and ten $A_1$ singularities ($m_i = 2$):
- Paper's formula: $\frac{5 \cdot 4}{2} + 10 \cdot \frac{2 \cdot 1}{2} = 10 + 10 = 20 \neq 0$  ✗
- Corrected formula: $10 - 10 = 0$  ✓

The proof of `lem:rational_sextic_ten_nodes` concludes "$p_a(C) = 0$ iff $i = 10$ and $m_i = 2$", which is only valid with the $-$ sign.

**Resolution**: Fixed in `sections/999_Appendix.tex` L14 by changing the $+$ sign before the summation to a $-$.

## 3. Cross-term exponent in $K_S^2$ computation

**Location**: `999_Appendix.tex` L22–23

**As written**: $$K_S^2 = (\pi^* K_{\mathbb{P}^2})^2 + \left(\sum E_k\right)^2 + \left(\sum \pi^* K_{\mathbb{P}^2} \cdot E_k\right)^{\mathbf{2}}$$

**Claim**: The exponent 2 on the cross-term is wrong; it should appear linearly.

**Proof**: Expanding $(A + B)^2$ where $A = \pi^* K_{\mathbb{P}^2}$ and $B = \sum E_k$: $$(A + B)^2 = A^2 + 2A \cdot B + B^2$$

The cross-term is $2A \cdot B = 2\sum_k \pi^* K_{\mathbb{P}^2} \cdot E_k$.
By the projection formula, $\pi^* D \cdot E_k = 0$ for any divisor $D$ on $\mathbb{P}^2$ and any exceptional divisor $E_k$.
So $2A \cdot B = 0$.
The paper instead writes this term as $(A \cdot B)^2 = 0^2 = 0$, arriving at the same value by coincidence.
The intermediate step is formally wrong: the binomial expansion of $(A+B)^2$ does not produce $(AB)^2$.

The final result $K_S^2 = 9 - 10 = -1$ is correct.

**Resolution**: Fixed in `sections/999_Appendix.tex` L23 by replacing the squared cross-term with the proper linear term $2\left(\sum_k \pi^*K_{\mathbb{P}^2} \cdot E_k\right)$.

## 4. Lattice isomorphism in `lem:cusp_map_dP`

**Location**: `030_Cusp_Correspondence.tex` L573

**As written**: $$\tilde w_1^{\perp T_{\mathrm{dP}}}/\tilde w_1 \cong (18, 0, 0)_1 \cong U \oplus E_8(2)$$

**Claim**: $U \oplus E_8(2)$ should be $U \oplus E_8^2$.

**Proof by rank**: The lattice $(18, 0, 0)_1$ has rank 18 by definition (the first Nikulin invariant is the rank).
$U \oplus E_8(2)$ has rank $2 + 8 = 10 \neq 18$.
$U \oplus E_8^2$ has rank $2 + 8 + 8 = 18$.
✓

**Cross-check with the proof**: The proof of this same lemma (L584–591) explicitly computes: $$\frac{(e')^{\perp T_{\mathrm{dP}}}}{e'} \cong \langle e, f \rangle \oplus \langle \alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_8 \rangle \oplus \langle \tilde\alpha_1, \ldots, \tilde\alpha_8 \rangle \cong U \oplus E_8^2$$

So the statement of the lemma contradicts its own proof.
The proof is correct.

**Resolution**: Fixed in `sections/030_Cusp_Correspondence.tex` L573 by changing $E_8(2)$ to $E_8^2$ in the lemma statement.

## 5. $K_S^2$ formula in Introduction

**Location**: `000_Introduction.tex` L45

**As written**: $K_S^2 = 9 - |\Sigma| - n$

**Claim**: Should be $K_S^2 = 9 - |\Sigma|$ (no extra $-n$).

**Proof by table check**: From the table at L50–62, verify the formula row by row:

| $n$ | $|\Sigma|$ | $K_S^2$ (table) | $9 - |\Sigma| - n$ | $9 - |\Sigma|$ |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 10 | $-1$ | $-2$ ✗ | $-1$ ✓ |
| 2 | 11 | $-2$ | $-4$ ✗ | $-2$ ✓ |
| 3 | 12 | $-3$ | $-6$ ✗ | $-3$ ✓ |

Every row fails with the extra $-n$.
The correct relation is $|\Sigma| = 9 + n$, yielding $K_S^2 = 9 - (9+n) = -n$, consistent with $K_S^2 = -n$ stated at L8 in the same file.

**Resolution**: Fixed in `sections/000_Introduction.tex` L45 by changing $K_S^2 = 9 - |\Sigma| - n$ to $K_S^2 = 9 - |\Sigma|$.

**Resolution**: Fixed in `sections/030_Cusp_Correspondence.tex` L47 by changing "$A_7$" to "$B_7$".

### 9. Invalid characterization of rational sextics — `999_Appendix.tex` `lem:rational_sextic_ten_nodes`

**Location**: `999_Appendix.tex` L7-17

**As written**:
> "Let $C$ be a generic irreducible sextic curve... $C$ is rational if and only if $C$ has precisely ten $A_1$ singularities... we immediately obtain $p_a(C) = 0$ if and only if $i=10$ and $m_i=2$ for all $i$."

**Claim**: The statement is mathematically false in both directions without specifying that the curve has "at worst ordinary double points".

**Proof**: The genus-degree formula gives the geometric genus $g = \frac{(6-1)(6-2)}{2} - \sum \delta_p = 10 - \sum \delta_p$.
A curve is rational iff $g=0$, which requires $\sum \delta_p = 10$.
1. **$\implies$**: A rational sextic does NOT need to have exactly 10 singularities or only $A_1$ singularities.
   For example, a rational sextic can have a $D_4$ singularity ($\delta \ge 3$) and 7 $A_1$ singularities, or 9 $A_1$ singularities and 1 $A_2$ singularity.
   Severi varieties of rational sextics include lower-dimensional strata with fewer, more degenerate singularities.
2. **$\impliedby$**: Saying a curve "has precisely 10 $A_1$ singularities" does not preclude it from having an 11th singularity elsewhere unless explicitly stated.
   Moreover, "generic irreducible sextic" usually implies a smooth sextic of genus 10, not a rationally $A_1$-singular one.

The formula actually only proves that solving $10 - \sum \delta_p = 0$ using exclusively $\delta_p=1$ ($A_1$ singularities) requires exactly 10 of them.

**Resolution**: Rewrote the lemma in `sections/999_Appendix.tex` L7 to state: "Let $C \subset \mathbb{P}^2$ be an irreducible sextic curve with at worst $A_1$ singularities.
Then $C$ is a rational curve if and only if it has exactly ten $A_1$ singularities."
Rewrote the proof to clearly bound the geometric genus as $g = 10 - k$, where $k$ is the number of $A_1$ singularities.
Additionally added `\cref{rmk:severi-sextics}` explaining that rational sextics with fewer $A_1$ singularities and worse singularities (e.g. $A_2$ or higher order) exist in the lower-dimensional strata of the Severi variety $V_{6,10}$.
