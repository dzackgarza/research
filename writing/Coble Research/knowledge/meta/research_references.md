## 1. Foundations of Coble Surfaces and Curves

- **D. Zack Garza (2024)**: *Complete Moduli Spaces of Coble Surfaces*.
    - **Relevance**: Current project manuscript / preprint. Describes the modular KSBA stable pair compactification for Coble surfaces and identifies it with a semitoroidal compactification.
- **Daniel Zachary Garza (2026)**: *Compact Moduli of Numerically Polarized Degree 2 Enriques Surfaces*.
    - **Relevance**: Garza's doctoral dissertation. Provides the foundational technical results for degree 2 numerical polarizations and the IAS construction on discs.
- **A. B. Coble (1917)**: *The ten nodes of the rational plane sextic*. American Journal of Mathematics, 39(2), 327–372.
    - **Relevance**: The original study of the "Coble curve" (rational plane sextic with 10 nodes) and its relationship to the blowup construction.
- **Igor Dolgachev and Serguei Kondyrev (2013)**: *The rationality of the moduli spaces of Coble surfaces and of nodal Enriques surfaces*. Izvestiya: Mathematics, 77 (3), 453–491.
    - **Relevance**: Defines the geometric lattices $S_{\mathrm{Co}}$ and $T_{\mathrm{Co}}$, the Horikawa model, and the relationship between Coble surfaces and K3 surfaces.
- **A. B. Coble (1929)**: *Algebraic Geometry and Theta Functions*. American Mathematical Society Colloquium Publications, Volume X.
    - **Relevance**: Foundational book on the geometry of configurations of points and their associated surfaces.
- **Chenglong Yu, Zhiwei Zheng, & Yiming Zhong (2025)**: *Moduli spaces of sextic curves with simple singularities and their compactifications*. arXiv:2505.16727.
    - **Relevance**: Studies moduli spaces of sextic curves with simple singularities via period maps to K3 surfaces with ADE singularities. Identifies GIT compactifications with Looijenga compactifications and describes Picard lattices. Establishes algebraic embeddings into arithmetic quotients of type IV domains.

## 2. Lattice Theory and Nikulin's Classification

- **F. Scattone (1987)**: *On the Compactification of Moduli Spaces for Algebraic K3 Surfaces*. Memoirs of the AMS.
    - **Relevance**: Foundational work on the BHC/BB compactification of K3 surfaces. Crucial for the study of primitive isotropic sublattices and quadratic forms in the context of K3 covers.
- **V. V. Nikulin (1979)**: *Integer symmetric bilinear forms and some of their geometric applications*. Izv. Akad. Nauk SSSR Ser. Mat., 43 (1), 111–177.
    - **Relevance**: Crucial for 2-elementary lattices, surjectivity of $O(L) \to O(q_L)$, the uniqueness of primitive embeddings, and the classification of isotropic vectors via the discriminant form.
- **V. V. Nikulin (1980)**: *Factor groups of the automorphism groups of hyperbolic forms by subgroup generated by 2-reflections*. Soviet Math. Dokl., 21 (1), 83–87.
    - **Relevance**: Foundations for the reflection group action $W(S)$ on the period domain.

## 3. Moduli of Enriques Surfaces and Orbits

- **V. Alexeev, P. Engel, D. Zack Garza, & M. Schaffler (2023)**: *Compact moduli of Enriques surfaces with a numerical polarization of degree 2*. arXiv:2312.03638.
    - **Relevance**: Primary ground truth for the Enriques sector. Modernizes flowerpots into slc stable pairs and describes the IAS construction.
- **H. Sterk (1991)**: *Compactifications of the period space of Enriques surfaces. I*. Mathematische Zeitschrift, 207(1), 1-36.
    - **Relevance**: Introduction of **Sterk's Technique** for determining $O(T)$-orbits of isotropic lines/planes by lifting from the discriminant group. Defines the numerical degree 2 polarization ($h^2=2$).
- **V. Gritsenko & K. Hulek (2016)**: *Moduli of polarized Enriques surfaces*.
    - **Relevance**: Discusses the finiteness and classification of moduli spaces of polarized Enriques surfaces for various degrees.
- **S. Kond\=o (1986)**: *Enriques surfaces II*.
    - **Relevance**: Detailed description of the period domain and the configuration of the BB boundaries.

## 4. Modern KSBA Moduli and Compactifications

- **V. Alexeev & P. Engel (2023)**: *Compact moduli of K3 surfaces*. Annals of Mathematics, 198(2), 727-789.
    - **Relevance**: Foundational for the KSBA approach to K3 moduli. Describes the semitoroidal and GIT compactifications.
- **V. Alexeev (2002)**: *Complete moduli in the presence of semiabelian group action*. Annals of Mathematics, 155(3), 611-708.
    - **Relevance**: Foundational K3 work on stable log varieties and their moduli spaces.
- **David R. Morrison (1981)**: *Semistable degenerations of Enriques’ and hyperelliptic surfaces*. Duke Math. J., 48 (1), 197–249.
    - **Relevance**: The original "Flowerpot" construction for semistable limits, which provides the starting point for the slc stable pair models.

## 5. Singularity and Degeneration

- **R. Friedman (1983)**: *Global smoothings of varieties with normal crossings*.
    - **Relevance**: General theory for the smoothing of the "Pot" and the behavior of the IAS near its boundary.
- **K. Kulikov (1977)**: *Degenerations of K3 surfaces and Enriques surfaces*.
    - **Relevance**: Classification of degenerations (Types I, II, III).
