> [!conjecture] No-moduli-loss in polarized Coble compactification The stable quotient should still remember the **marked Coble root**. Otherwise the boundary fan could be coarsened too far and the comparison theorem would fail.

## Statement

- The stable quotient is supposed to retain enough information to distinguish branches that differ by the marked Coble root.
- In the Talks framework, the geometric memory is often described by the singular point of type $\frac{1}{4}(1,1)$ or, on the resolution side, by the contracted anti-bicanonical $(-4)$-curve.
- A more explicit source-local version is the "maximal-double-curve" claim: strict coarsening of the boundary fan would identify geometrically distinct degenerations even though the stable pair still remembers that singular point / contracted curve package.

## Why it matters

- Without this memory, different degenerations could collapse to the same stable quotient.
- That would make the restricted semifan too fine for the actual KSBA boundary and would destroy the intended comparison with the semitoroidal model.

## Relation to other questions

- Root-orbit uniqueness controls the branch structure before compactification.
- No-moduli-loss controls whether the compactified stable models still remember that branch structure.
- The generic one-fixed-point picture is not enough near the boundary closure; one still has to show that normalization of the Heegner divisor separates the multiple torus-fixed-point branches appearing there.
