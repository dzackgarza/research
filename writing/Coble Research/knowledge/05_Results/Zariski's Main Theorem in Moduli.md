> [!theorem] Application of Zariski's Main Theorem Let $f: X \to Y$ be a birational, finite morphism between normal varieties, with $Y$ proper.
> Then $f$ is an isomorphism.
> 
> For the Enriques compactification, the classifying map $\phi: B^\nu \to \overline{F_{{\mathrm{En}}, 2}}^\nu$ is:
> 1. **Birational**: an isomorphism on the interior $F_{{\mathrm{En}}, 2}$.
> 2. **Finite**: proved via the "no coarsening" semifan argument.
> 3. **Normal**: both spaces are normal by construction.
> 4. **Proper**: KSBA stacks are proper.
> 
> Thus, $\phi$ is an isomorphism, identifying the geometric KSBA limits with the explicitly constructible semitoroidal boundary.
