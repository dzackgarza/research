> [!theorem] Finiteness of the Classifying Map $\phi$
> The classifying morphism $\phi: B^\nu \to \overline{F_{{\mathrm{En}}, 2}}$ is finite.
> 
> This is proved via a combinatorial comparison.
> The normal KSBA compactification induces its own semifans $\mathcal{G}_k$ which are coarsenings of the folded Coxeter semifans $\mathcal{F}_k$.
> The map is finite if and only if no coarsening occurs ($\mathcal{G}_k = \mathcal{F}_k$).
> 
> If a strict coarsening occurred, distinct maximal degenerations (differing by a double curve) would be identified in the target.
> However, the dual complex uniquely identifies maximally degenerate limits in KSBA theory.
> Thus, no such coarsening occurs.
