> [!theorem] Orbit Classification in $T = U \oplus \overline{T}_\eta$ (Sterk 1991)
> Fix an even [[Lattice]] $T$ with a splitting $T = U \oplus \overline{T}_\eta$ with $\overline{T}$ negative-definite and even. Let $\eta_1, \eta_2 \in T$ be primitive isotropic vectors satisfying:
> 1. $\eta_1^2 = \eta_2^2 = k$
> 2. $\operatorname{div}_T(\eta_1) = \operatorname{div}_T(\eta_2) = p > 0$
> 3. $\eta_1 \equiv \eta_2 \pmod{pT}$
> 
> Then there exists an isometry $\phi \in \operatorname{O}^*(T)$ such that $\phi(\eta_1) = \eta_2$.


This theorem generalized the Eichler transvection method to classify primitive isotropic vectors and show that all divisibility-one vectors in $T_{\mathrm{En}}$ are in the same orbit.
