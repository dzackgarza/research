> [!definition] Recognizable Divisors
> A polarizing divisor $R$ on the generic surface in $F_S$ is **recognizable** if, for any quasipolarized Kulikov degeneration $\mathcal{X} \to \Delta$, $R$ extends unambiguously to a flat limit $R_0 \subset \mathcal{X}_0$, unique up to $\operatorname{Aut}^0(\tilde{\mathcal{X}}_0)$ in any other smoothing.
> 
> If $R$ is recognizable, the normalization of the KSBA compactification $\overline{F}^R$ is isomorphic to a semitoroidal compactification $\overline{F_S}^{\mathcal{F}_R}$ defined by a specific semifan $\mathcal{F}_R$.
> 
> **Examples**:
> 1. The **rational curve divisor** $R_{\mathrm{rc}} = \sum R_i$ (sum of all smooth rational curves in $|L|$).
> 2. The **ramification divisor** $R_\iota$ of a nonsymplectic involution.
