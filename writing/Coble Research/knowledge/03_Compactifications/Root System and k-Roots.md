> [!definition] Root System and k-Roots Let $L$ be a nondegenerate integral [[Lattice]] and $v\in L$ a primitive vector.
> The reflection in $v$ is an isometry of $L_{\mathbf{Q}}$, but is integral ($s_v \in \operatorname{O}(L)$) precisely when $2(v, L) \subseteq v^2 \mathbf{Z}$.
> 
> We define the **$k$-Weyl group** $W^k(L)$ as the group of reflections $s_v$ such that $s_v\in \operatorname{O}(L)$ is integral and $v^2 = -k$.
> The full **Weyl group** $W(L)$ is generated by integral reflections in vectors of any norm.
> 
> We say $v$ is a **$k$-root** if $s_v\in W^k(L)$.
> For [[2-Elementary Lattices|2-elementary lattices]], roots are precisely those vectors $v\in L$ with either:
> - $v^2 = -2$ (**short roots**), or
> - $v^2 = -4$ and $\operatorname{div}_L(v) = 2$ (**long roots**).
