> [!remark] Scattone's 1987 F_{2d} Paper: Compactification of Moduli Spaces for Algebraic K3 Surfaces Scattone's 1987 memoir focuses on **lattice-theoretic classification** of boundary components for the Baily-Borel compactification $\overline{F_{2d}}^{\operatorname{BB}}$.
> 
> **Core Methods:**
> - **Discriminant Form Analysis**: Systematically classifying boundary components using discriminant quadratic forms.
> - **Isotropic Sublattice Classification**: Establishing bijective correspondence between boundary components and $\Gamma_{2d}$-orbits of primitive isotropic sublattices of $T_{2d}$.
> - **Arithmetic Group Theory**: Applying the Eichler criterion and properties of $\operatorname{O}^+(T_{2d})$.
> 
> For $d=1$, Scattone uses embeddings into the 24 Niemeier lattices to classify the four types of rank-18 boundary lattices.
> The paper is primarily combinatorial and arithmetic, without extensive Hodge-theoretic machinery.
