> [!remark] Scattone's Method: Enumerating Boundary Components via Niemeier Lattices
> Scattone (1987) provides a concrete arithmetic approach to enumerating $0$-cusps in the Baily--Borel compactification $\overline{F_{2d}}^{\operatorname{BB}}$. 
> 
> $0$-cusps correspond to $\Gamma_{2d}$-orbits of primitive isotropic lines $I \subset L_{2d}$. Each such cusp corresponds to a degeneration of [[K3 surface|K3 surfaces]] with associated lattice $I^\perp / I$, encoding the limiting Hodge structure.
> 
> Scattone classified boundary components by studying primitive embeddings $U \hookrightarrow L$ where $L$ is one of the $24$ **Niemeier lattices** (even, negative-definite, unimodular lattices of rank $24$). The orthogonal complement $T \mathrel{\mathop:}= U^{\perp_L}$ is an even, negative-definite, unimodular lattice of rank $22$. The enumeration of $0$-cusps reduces to counting orbits of primitive embeddings $U \hookrightarrow L for each Niemeier lattice up to $\operatorname{O}(L)$.
