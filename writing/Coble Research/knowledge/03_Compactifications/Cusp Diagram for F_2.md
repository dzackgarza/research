> [!example] Cusp Diagram for $F_2$ (Scattone)
> For $d=1$, there is exactly one $\Gamma_2$-orbit of primitive isotropic lines in $T_2$, yielding a unique 0-cusp (Type III) in $\overline{F_2}^{\operatorname{BB}}$.
> 
> There are four $\Gamma_2$-orbits of primitive isotropic planes in $T_2$, yielding four Type II boundary curves.
> The corresponding boundary lattices $\overline{T}_I$ are rank 18 negative-definite lattices distinguished by their root sublattices:
> - $A_1 \oplus E_8^{\oplus 2}$
> - $E_7 \oplus D_{10}$
> - $A_1 \oplus D_{16}$
> - $A_{17}$
> 
> All four curves meet transversely at the unique Type III point.
