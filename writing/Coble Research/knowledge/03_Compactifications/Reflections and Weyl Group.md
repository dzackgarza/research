> [!definition] Reflections and Weyl Group
> Let $(L,\beta)$ be a nondegenerate integral [[Lattice]]. For an anisotropic vector $\alpha \in L$ satisfying the condition $2\beta(\alpha, L) \subseteq \beta(\alpha,\alpha)\mathbf{Z}$, the **reflection in $\alpha$** is the isometry: $$ s_\alpha(x) \mathrel{\mathop:}= x - 2\frac{\beta(\alpha,x)}{\beta(\alpha,\alpha)}\alpha $$ Reflections have determinant -1 and act as the identity on the orthogonal complement $\langle \alpha \rangle^{\perp L}$.
> 
> The group generated by all such reflections is the **Weyl group** $W(L) \leq \operatorname{O}(L)$.
