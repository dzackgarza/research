> [!example] Examples of Classical Foldings
> Folding produces scaled root systems in the invariant [[Lattice|lattice]]. Explicit examples computed via [[Folded Root Systems]]:
> - **$A_5 \to C_3(2)$**: Under horizontal reflection ($G=S_2$), $\Phi(A_5^G)$ has Gram matrix equal to $G_{C_3(2)} = 2 \cdot G_{C_3}$.
> - **$D_4 \to C_3$**: Under vertical reflection ($G=S_2$), $\Phi(D_4^G)$ has Gram matrix equal to $G_{C_3}$.
> - **$D_4 \to G_2$**: Under rotation by $2\pi/3$ ($G=\mathbf{Z}/3\mathbf{Z}$), $\Phi(D_4^G)$ has Gram matrix equal to $G_{G_2}$.
> - **$E_6 \to F_4(2)$**: Under horizontal reflection ($G=S_2$), $\Phi(E_6^G)$ has Gram matrix equal to $G_{F_4(2)}$.
