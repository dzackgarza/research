> [!table] Sterk cusp divisibilities The five 0-cusps of $F_{\mathrm{En},2}$ can be represented by primitive isotropic vectors with the following divisibilities:

| Sterk cusp | Vector | $\operatorname{div}*{T*{\mathrm{En}}}$ | $\operatorname{div}*{T*{\mathrm{K3}}}$ |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | $e$ | 1 | 1 |
| 2 | $e'$ | 2 | 2 |
| 3 | $e' + f' + \overline{\alpha}_8$ | 2 | 1 |
| 4 | $2e' + f' + \overline{\alpha}_1$ | 2 | 1 |
| 5 | $2e + 2f + \overline{\alpha}_1$ | 2 | 1 |

## Use in the Coble paper

- **Sterk cusp 2** is the one matched by the Coble 0-cusp under the map
  $$
  (9,9,1)_1 \mapsto (18,0,0)_1.
  $$
- The divisibility data distinguishes the boundary components and is used to identify the correct orbit of isotropic vectors in the Enriques and degree-2 K3 moduli spaces [@Ste91].
