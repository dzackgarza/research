> [!definition] Discriminant Locus and Weyl Chambers
> The **discriminant locus** $\Delta(L)$ is the union of all mirrors (hyperplanes orthogonal to roots): $$ \Delta(L) \mathrel{\mathop:}= \bigcup_{v \in \Phi_2(L)} v^{\perp L} $$
> 
> The **Weyl chambers** are the connected components of $C_L^{\circ} \mathrel{\mathop:}= C_L \setminus \Delta(L)$ where $C_L$ is the cone of positive-norm vectors in $L$.
> A **simple system** is a set of roots whose mirrors form the bounding hyperplanes for a fundamental domain for the action of $W(L)$.
