> [!question] Admissibility for polarized Coble cusps
> Which roots at a polarized Coble cusp are **admissible**, meaning compatible with the marked Coble orbit and still visible after passing through the folded K3-to-Enriques boundary data?

## Proposed criterion

- The derivative report cluster formulates admissibility by lifting a candidate cusp root back to a primitive $(-2)$-root in the ambient Enriques lattice that belongs to the designated Coble orbit.
- This is the extra datum that turns plain Enriques cusp data into polarized Coble cusp data.
- The same marked root must stay fixed when passing from a 0-cusp pair $(I,r)$ to a 1-cusp pair $(J,r)$, so admissibility is part of the incidence bookkeeping rather than a separate afterthought.

## Why it matters

- Admissibility controls which restricted walls survive in the induced semifan.
- It also explains why simple transfer of Enriques cusp diagrams is not enough: the marked Coble root changes the boundary problem.

## Remaining questions

- The precise admissibility test still needs a clean final formulation against the folded K3-to-Enriques Coxeter data.
- The Talks material repeatedly flags Sterk cusps 3 and 5 as delicate places where extra reflection data may matter.
