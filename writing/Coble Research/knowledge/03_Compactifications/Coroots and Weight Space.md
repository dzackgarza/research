> [!definition] Coroots and Weight Space
> If $R \mathrel{\mathop:}= \langle \Phi \rangle_{\mathbf{Z}}$ is a [[Root lattice|root lattice]] generated by a root system $\Phi$, the [[Dual Lattice|dual lattice]] $R^{\vee}$ is referred to as the **weight space** of $R$, and the dual basis elements $w_i$ defined by $w_i(\alpha_j) = \delta_{ij}$ are referred to as **coroots**.

For unimodular lattices such as $E_8$, it is often useful to identify $E_8^{\vee} \cong E_8$ and work in a basis of coroots $w_1, \ldots, w_8 \mathrel{\mathop:}= \alpha_1^{\vee}, \ldots, \alpha_8^{\vee}$.
The coordinates of each $w_i$ in terms of the original roots $\alpha_i$ can be found explicitly using the inverse Gram matrix.
