> [!definition] Baily-Borel Compactification For a Hermitian symmetric domain $D$ and an arithmetic group $\Gamma \le \operatorname{Aut}(D)$, the **Baily-Borel compactification** $\overline{F_\Gamma}^{\operatorname{BB}}$ of $F_\Gamma = \Gamma \backslash D$ is defined as $\operatorname{Proj} A(\Gamma)$, where $A(\Gamma)$ is the graded algebra of automorphic forms.
> 
> It is the unique minimal normal projective compactification.
> The boundary $\partial \overline{F_\Gamma}^{\operatorname{BB}}$ is stratified by rational boundary components, which correspond to maximal rational parabolic subgroups of $\Gamma$.
> For Type IV domains $D_T$ associated to a lattice $T$, the boundary components are in bijection with $\Gamma$-orbits of primitive isotropic sublattices of $T$.
> 
> While canonical and functorial, the boundary is typically highly singular and of high codimension, often not providing a modular interpretation for degenerate limits.
