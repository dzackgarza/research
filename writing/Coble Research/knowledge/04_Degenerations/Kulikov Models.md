> [!definition] Kulikov Models
> A **Kulikov model** is a degeneration ${\mathcal{X}} \to \Delta$ of K3 surfaces where:
> 1. The total space ${\mathcal{X}}$ is regular (smooth as a threefold).
> 2. The central fiber ${\mathcal{X}}_0$ is a reduced simple normal crossings (snc) divisor.
> 3. The relative dualizing sheaf is trivial: $\omega_{{\mathcal{X}}/\Delta} \cong {\mathcal{O}}_{{\mathcal{X}}}$.
> 
> Depending on the nilpotency index of the log monodromy operator $N = \log T$, where $T$ is the unipotent Picard-Lefschetz monodromy, Kulikov models are classified into three types:
> 
> - **Type I** ($N=0$): ${\mathcal{X}}_0$ is a smooth K3 surface.
> - **Type II** ($N^2=0, N \neq 0$): ${\mathcal{X}}_0$ is a chain of surfaces glued along elliptic curves. The dual complex is an interval $\mathbf{IAD}^1$.
> - **Type III** ($N^3=0, N^2 \neq 0$): ${\mathcal{X}}_0$ is a union of rational surfaces glued along rational curves, forming an anticanonical cycle on each component. The dual complex is a sphere $\mathrm{IAS}^2$.
> 
> The **weight filtration** $W_\bullet$ on $H^2({\mathcal{X}}_t; \mathbf{C})$ reflects these types. For Type I, only $W_2$ is non-trivial. For Type II, $W_0$ and $W_2$ appear. For Type III, all even weight pieces ($W_0, W_2, W_4$) are non-trivial.
