> [!definition] Half-Divisor Models A **half-divisor model** is a pair $(\mathcal{Z}, \mathcal{R}_{\mathcal{Z}})$ over a base curve $C$ that arises as the quotient of a divisor model $(\mathcal{X}, \mathcal{R}) \to C$ by a fixed-point-free involution $\tau$ where $\mathcal{R}$ is anti-invariant ($\tau^*\mathcal{R} = -\mathcal{R}$).
> 
> $\mathcal{R}*{\mathcal{Z}}$ is a Weil divisor that is not Cartier, but $2\mathcal{R}*{\mathcal{Z}}$ is Cartier.
> This naturally models limits of polarized Enriques surfaces, where the polarization does not descend to a Cartier divisor on the Enriques quotient.
> Boundary strata for Enriques stable limits are modeled by pairs $(\mathcal{Z}*0, \frac{1}{2}\mathcal{R}*{\mathcal{Z}*0})$ with $K*{\mathcal{Z}*0} + \frac{1}{2}\mathcal{R}*{\mathcal{Z}_0}$ ample and slc singularities.
