> [!remark] Morrison flowerpot degenerations for Coble surfaces
> Certain degenerations of Coble surfaces fit the classical flowerpot picture for Enriques degenerations: a central fiber is assembled from flowers, stalks, and a pot component, and the resulting period lies on the Coble divisor inside Enriques moduli [@Ols04; @Sha81; @AEGS23].

## Relevance to Coble surfaces

- A **flower** is a pair $(\mathbf{P}^2, C)$ with $C^2=4$ [@Ols04; @Sha81].
- A **stalk** is modeled by a ruled surface of type $\Sigma_4$ with boundary sections of self-intersection $4$ and $-4$ [@Ols04; @Sha81].
- A **pot** is a rational surface with boundary divisor built from $(-4)$-curves, matching the Coble anti-bicanonical geometry [@Ols04; @Sha81].

## Moduli interpretation

- These are Type I semistable degenerations on the Enriques side with klt singularities [@Ols04; @AEGS23].
- The Coble boundary is expected to correspond to disk-type integral-affine structures rather than sphere- or $\mathbf{RP}^2$-type limits [@AEGS23].
- In the modern KSBA picture, the flowerpot language is replaced by dlt and slc stable-pair models, but it still provides the geometric intuition for the Coble boundary behavior [@Ols04; @AEGS23].
