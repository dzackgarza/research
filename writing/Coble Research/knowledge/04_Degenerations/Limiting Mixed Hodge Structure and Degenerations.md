> [!theorem] Limiting Mixed Hodge Structure and Degenerations For a one-parameter degeneration of polarized K3 surfaces over a punctured disk, the unipotent monodromy operator $T \in O(T_{2d})$ and its nilpotent logarithm $N = \log T$ induce a canonical monodromy weight filtration $W^{\bullet}$.
> 
> The boundary components of $\overline{F_{2d}}^{\operatorname{BB}}$ are classified by the nilpotency index of $N$:
> - **Type II degenerations (1-cusps)**: $N \neq 0, N^2 = 0$.
>   The weight filtration is 3-step ($W_0 \subset W_1 \subset W_2 \subset W_3$), and $\operatorname{Gr}_2^W = I^\perp / I = \overline{T}_I$ carries a pure polarized Hodge structure of weight 2.
> - **Type III degenerations (0-cusps)**: $N^2 \neq 0, N^3 = 0$.
>   The weight filtration is of maximal length.
>   These correspond to normal crossing varieties whose dual complex is a triangulation of $S^2$.
