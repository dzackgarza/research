> [!definition] Singularities in the Minimal Model Program
> Let $(X, D)$ be a normal pair with $K_X + D$ $\mathbf{Q}$-Cartier. For a log resolution $f: Y \to X$, the discrepancy $a(E, X, D)$ is defined by $K_Y + D_Y = f^*(K_X + D) + \sum a(E, X, D) E$.
> 
> Classes of singularities:
> - **Terminal**: $a(E, X, D) > 0$.
> - **Canonical**: $a(E, X, D) \ge 0$.
> - **Kawamata log terminal (klt)**: $a(E, X, D) > -1$.
> - **Log canonical (lc)**: $a(E, X, D) \ge -1$. (Maximal class where MMP works).
> 
> For non-normal varieties:
> - **Demi-normal**: Reduced, $S_2$, normal crossings in codimension 1.
> - **Semi-log canonical (slc)**: $X$ is demi-normal, and the normalization with the conductor divisor $(X^\nu, D^\nu)$ is log canonical. This is the condition required for limits in KSBA moduli spaces.
