> [!definition] Divisor Model A **divisor model** for a degeneration $\pi: {\mathcal{X}} \to C$ of K3 (or Enriques) surfaces is a degeneration of pairs $({\mathcal{X}}, {\mathcal{R}}) \to C$ where:
> 1. ${\mathcal{R}}$ is a Cartier divisor, effective on all fibers.
> 2. For $t \neq 0$, ${\mathcal{R}}_t$ is semiample.
> 3. ${\mathcal{R}}$ is disjoint from the singular strata of the central fiber ${\mathcal{X}}_0$.
> 
> The geometry is encoded by an **integral-affine divisor** $R_{\mathrm{IA}} \subset \Gamma({\mathcal{X}}_0)$, which assigns weights to the double curves reflecting the intersection with ${\mathcal{R}}_0$, satisfying a balancing condition at every vertex.
> 
> A **half-divisor model** arises when $({\mathcal{X}}, {\mathcal{R}})$ admits an Enriques involution preserving ${\mathcal{R}}$.
> The quotient $({\mathcal{Z}}, {\mathcal{R}}*{\mathcal{Z}}) = ({\mathcal{X}}, {\mathcal{R}}) / \iota*{{\mathrm{En}}}$ is a half-divisor model.
> Its KSBA stable limit can be computed directly from the relative Proj of its section ring.
