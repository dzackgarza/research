> [!example] $B_n$ and $C_n$ root systems The root system $R(B_n) \subset \mathbf{Z}^n$ consists of all vectors of the form $r_i \mathrel{\mathop:}= \pm e_i$ for $1\leq i \leq n$ and $r_{ij}\mathrel{\mathop:}= \pm e_i \pm e_j$ for $1\leq i < j \leq n$.
> - There are $2n^2$ total roots ($2n$ short roots $r_i$ and $2n(n-1)$ long roots $r_{ij}$).
> - $\Phi(B_n)$ consists of roots $\alpha_i \mathrel{\mathop:}= e_i - e_{i+1}$ for $1\leq i \leq n-1$ and $\alpha_n = e_n$.
> 
> The root system $R(C_n)$ consists of the roots $2r_i$ and $r_{ij}$ as in type $B$.
> - There are $2n^2$ total roots ($2n$ long roots $2r_i$ and $2n(n-1)$ short roots $r_{ij}$).
> - $\Phi(C_n)$ consists of $\alpha_i \mathrel{\mathop:}= e_i - e_{i+1}$ for $1\leq i \leq n-1$ and $\alpha_n = 2e_n$.
