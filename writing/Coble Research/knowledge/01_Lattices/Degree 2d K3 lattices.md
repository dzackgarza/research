> [!definition] Degree 2d K3 lattices
> We define the family of lattices
> $$ L_{\mathrm{K3}, 2d}^{(m)} \mathrel{\mathop:}= \langle-2 d\rangle \oplus U^2 \oplus E_8^m, \qquad  L_{\mathrm{K3}, 2d} \mathrel{\mathop:}= L_{\mathrm{K3}, 2d}^{(2)} $$
> These are the degree 2d K3 lattices that appear in the study of the moduli spaces $F_{2d}$ of [[K3 surface|K3 surfaces]] with a polarization of degree $2d$. 
> 
> The lattice $L_{\mathrm{K3}, 2d}$, corresponding to the $m=2$ case, models the orthogonal complement in the [[K3 lattice]] $L_{\mathrm{K3}}$ of a polarization $h$ of degree $2d$.
