> [!definition] Eichler-Siegel Transformations (Transvections)
> Let $e \in L_{\mathbf{Q}}$ be an isotropic vector and let $a \in \langle e \rangle^{\perp_{L_{\mathbf{Q}}}}$ be any vector satisfying $a\cdot e=0$.
> The **Seigel-Eichler transformation** associated to the pair $(e, a)$ is the isometry $E_{e,a} \in \operatorname{O}(L_{\mathbf{Q}})$ defined by: $$ E_{e,a}(v) = v + \beta(e, v)a - ( \beta(a, v) + \frac{1}{2}\beta(a, a)\beta(e, v) ) e $$ If $a$ is also isotropic, this simplifies to $E_{e,a}(v) = v + \beta(e, v)a - \beta(a, v)e$.
> 
> The **Eichler transvection group** $E(L)$ is generated by all such transvections where $e \in L$ is a primitive isotropic vector with $\operatorname{div}_L(e) = 1$, and $a \in \langle e \rangle^{\perp_L}$.
