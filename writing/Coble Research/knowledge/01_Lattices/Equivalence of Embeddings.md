> [!definition] Equivalence of Embeddings
> Two primitive embeddings $\iota_1\mathrel{\Big|} S \hookrightarrow L_1$ and $\iota_2\mathrel{\Big|} S \hookrightarrow L_2$ are **equivalent** if there exists an isometry $f \in \operatorname{Isom}(L_1, L_2)$ such that $f \circ \iota_1 = \iota_2$.
> 
> If $L_1 = L_2 = L$ is a single lattice, two primitive embeddings $\iota_1, \iota_2: S \hookrightarrow L$ are **equivalent** if they are equivalent by an element of the orthogonal group $\operatorname{O}(L)$.
> The set of equivalence classes of primitive embeddings of $S$ into $L$ is denoted $\operatorname{Emb}(S, L)$.
