> [!definition] Hyperbolic plane The **hyperbolic plane** $U$ is the unique even, unimodular [[Lattice]] of signature $(1,1)$, with a standard basis given by vectors $\{e, f\}$ satisfying $e^2 = f^2 = 0$, $e \cdot f = 1$.
> Its [[Gram Matrix|Gram matrix]] is $$ G_U = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} $$

## Properties

- The full set of primitive isotropic vectors is $\{\pm e, \pm f\}$.
- The orthogonal group of $U$ is $\operatorname{O}(U) = \{\pm\mathrm{id}, \pm\begin{bsmallmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bsmallmatrix}\} \cong C_2^2$, a Klein four group generated by the involutions $(e,f) \mapsto (-e, -f)$ and $(e,f)\mapsto (f, e)$.
- The rational orthogonal group $\operatorname{O}(U_{\mathbf{Q}})$ is much larger, containing one-parameter subgroups $i_a = \begin{pmatrix} a & 0 \\ 0 & a^{-1} \end{pmatrix}$ and $j_b = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & b \\ b^{-1} & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
- The scaled lattice $U(2)$ has $U(2)^{\vee} = \frac{1}{2}U$, and $A_{U(2)} \cong (\mathbf{Z}/2\mathbf{Z})^2$.
