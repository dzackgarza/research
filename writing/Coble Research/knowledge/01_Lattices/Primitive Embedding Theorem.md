> [!theorem] Primitive Embedding Theorem
> Let $S$ be a primitive non-degenerate sublattice of a unimodular lattice $L$, and let $T = S^{\perp L}$ be its orthogonal complement.
> Then:
> 1. $S \oplus T$ is a sublattice of $L$ of finite index.
> 2. $[L\colon S \oplus T] = |A_S| = |A_T|$, where $A_S$ and $A_T$ are the [[Discriminant Group and Discriminant|discriminant groups]].
> 3. There is a canonical isomorphism $\psi: A_S \to A_T$ such that the [[Discriminant Forms|discriminant forms]] are related by an **anti-isometry**: $$ q_{A_T}(\psi(x)) = -q_{A_S}(x) \qquad \forall x\in A_S $$
