> [!definition] Ordinary and Characteristic Vectors
> For [[2-Elementary Lattices|2-elementary lattices]], one always has $\operatorname{div}_{L}(v) \in\{1,2\}$.
> We set $v^* \mathrel{\mathop:}= v / \operatorname{div}_{L}(v) \in A_{L}$.
> 
> Letting $q_{L}: A_{L} \to \frac{1}{2} \mathbf{Z} / \mathbf{Z}$ be the induced quadratic form on $A_{L}$, we say $v^*$ is **characteristic** if $q_{L}(x)=\beta_{L}(v^*, x) \bmod \mathbf{Z}$ for all $x \in A_{L}$, and is **ordinary** otherwise.
> 
> We say that a primitive isotropic vector $v \in L$ is:
> - **odd** if $\operatorname{div}_{L}(v)=1$,
> - **even ordinary** if $\operatorname{div}_{L}(v)=2$ and $v^*$ is ordinary, or
> - **even characteristic** if $\operatorname{div}_{L}(v)=2$ and $v^*$ is characteristic.
