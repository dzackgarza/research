> [!definition] Type $\mathrm{II}$ Unimodular Lattices
> For $p, q \in \mathbf{Z}_{\geq 0}$, define
> $$ \mathrm{II}_{p, q} = \begin{cases} E_8^{\frac{p-q}{8}} \oplus U^q, & p-q \geq 0 \text{ and } 8|(p-q) \\ E_8^{\frac{q-p}{8}} \oplus U^p, & p-q < 0 \text{ and } 8|(q-p) \end{cases} $$
> where $E_8$ is the negative-definite $E_8$ lattice and $U$ is the [[Hyperbolic plane|hyperbolic lattice]].
> 
> This is an integral, even, unimodular [[Lattice]] of rank $p+q$ and signature $(p, q)$ with discriminant $(-1)^p$.


By classification, if $L$ is an even indefinite unimodular lattice with $\operatorname{sig}(L) = (p, q)$, then necessarily $\tau \equiv 0\pmod{8\mathbf{Z}}$ and $L \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{II}_{p, q}$.
