> [!definition] Discriminant Forms
> For a nondegenerate *even* [[Lattice]] $(L,\beta)$, the **discriminant bilinear form** is $\beta_{A_L}: A_L \times A_L \to \mathbf{Q}/\mathbf{Z}$, given by $\beta_{A_L}(\overline{x}, \overline{y}) = \beta(x,y) \bmod \mathbf{Z}$ for any lifts $x,y \in L^{\vee}$ and $\beta$ the induced form on $L^{\vee}$. 
> 
> It admits an associated **quadratic form** $q_{A_L}(\overline{x}) = \beta(x,x)\pmod{\mathbf{Z}} \in \mathbf{Q}/\mathbf{Z}$ for any lift $x$. (Note: another convention uses $q_{A_L}(\overline{x}) \mathrel{\mathop:}= \beta(x,x)\pmod{2\mathbf{Z}}\in \mathbf{Q}/2\mathbf{Z}$; these agree via the isomorphism $\mathbf{Q}/\mathbf{Z} \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathbf{Q}/2\mathbf{Z}$ induced by multiplication by $2$.)
> 
> We define its **orthogonal group** $\operatorname{O}(A_L)$ as the automorphisms that preserve $q_{A_L}$. 
> 
> The **length** $\ell(L)$ of $L$ is the minimal number of generators for the abelian group $A_L$.
