> [!definition] Type $\mathrm{I}$ Unimodular Lattices
> For any positive integer $n$ and non-negative integers $0 \leq p, q \leq n$ with $p+q=n$, let $L \cong \mathbf{Z}^n$ be generated by $p+q$ elements where the [[Gram Matrix|Gram matrix]] is diagonal with $p$ copies of +1 and $q$ copies of $-1$: $$ G_\beta = \begin{bmatrix} \mathrm{id}_{p \times p} & 0 \\ 0 & -\mathrm{id}_{q \times q} \end{bmatrix} $$ We write this lattice as $\mathrm{I}_{p, q} \mathrel{\mathop:}= \langle 1 \rangle^p \oplus \langle -1 \rangle^q$.
> 
> This is an integral, odd, unimodular [[Lattice]] of rank $p+q$ and signature $(p, q)$ with discriminant $(-1)^q$.

By the classification theorem of such lattices, if $L$ is any odd indefinite unimodular lattice with $\operatorname{sig}(L)=(p, q)$, then $L \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{I}_{p, q}$.
