> [!definition] K3 lattice
> The **K3 lattice** $L_{\mathrm{K3}}$ is given by 
> $$ L_{\mathrm{K3}} = U^3 \oplus E_8^2 \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{II}_{3, 19} $$
> where $U$ is the [[Hyperbolic plane]] and $E_8$ is the negative-definite $E_8$ [[Root lattice]].
> 
> It is the unique even unimodular lattice of signature $(3, 19)$ up to isometry.


## Role in Moduli Theory
For any [[K3 surface]] $X$, there exists a **marking** $H^2(X,\mathbf{Z}) \xrightarrow{\sim} L_{\mathrm{K3}}$ that identifies the cohomology lattice with this fixed reference lattice. Moduli spaces of polarized and marked K3 surfaces are typically governed by the Neron-Severi lattice $S_X$ and its orthogonal complement, the transcendental lattice $T_X$.
