> [!definition] Scaled Lattices For any [[Lattice]] $(L,\beta)$ and positive integer $m$, the **scaled lattice** $L(m)$ is the same $\mathbf{Z}$-module $L$ equipped with the bilinear form $\beta_m(v,w) = m \cdot \beta(v,w)$.

## Properties

- The signature of $L(m)$ is the same as $L$.
- The discriminant scales as $\operatorname{disc}(L(m)) = m^{\operatorname{rank}(L)} \cdot \operatorname{disc}(L)$.
- If $L$ is unimodular and $m>1$, $L(m)$ is not unimodular.
