> [!example] Diagonal and Hyperbolic Lattices
> The **diagonal lattice** $\langle a_1, \ldots, a_n \rangle$ is $\mathbf{Z}^n$ with the bilinear form $\beta(x,y) = \sum a_i x_i y_i$ and diagonal [[Gram Matrix|Gram matrix]] $\operatorname{diag}(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$. 
> 
> In the special case $a_1,\cdots, a_{p} = 1$ and $a_{m+1}, \cdots, a_n = -1$, we write this lattice as $\mathrm{I}_{p, q}$, due to its distinguished nature as the unique nondegenerate odd unimodular lattice of signature $p, q$.
> 
> The **hyperbolic lattice** $U$ (also known as the hyperbolic plane) is the free $\mathbf{Z}$-module $\mathbf{Z}^2$ with basis $e,f$ such that $\beta(e,e) = \beta(f,f) = 0$ and $\beta(e,f) = 1$. It is an even, integral, rank 2 lattice with Gram matrix 
> $$ G_U = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} $$
