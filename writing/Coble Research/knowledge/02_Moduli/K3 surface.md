> [!definition] K3 surface A **K3 surface** is a smooth, projective, geometrically integral surface $X$ over $k=\mathbf{C}$ such that its canonical bundle $\omega_X \cong \mathcal{O}_X$ is trivial, and its irregularity is $q(X) \mathrel{\mathop:}= h^{0, 1}(X) \mathrel{\mathop:}= H^0(\mathcal{O}_X) = 0$.

## Properties

- The [[K3 lattice]] $L_{\mathrm{K3}}$ models the integral cohomology $H^2(X; \mathbf{Z})$ of a K3 surface.
- A marked K3 surface is one equipped with an isometry $H^2(X, \mathbf{Z}) \xrightarrow{\sim} L_{\mathrm{K3}}$.
- Its Hodge diamond has $h^{1,1} = 20$, $h^{2,0} = h^{0,2} = 1$, and all other non-trivial Hodge numbers vanishing except $h^{0,0} = h^{2,2} = 1$.
