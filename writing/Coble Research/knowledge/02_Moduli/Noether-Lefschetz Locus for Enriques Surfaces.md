> [!definition] Noether-Lefschetz Locus $\mathrm{NL}_{T_{\mathrm{En}}}$
> The canonical double cover $\pi: X \to Z$ of a degree-2 polarized Enriques surface yields a K3 surface with two commuting involutions: the fixed-point-free Enriques involution $\iota_{{\mathrm{En}}}$ and the del Pezzo involution $\iota_{\operatorname{dP}}$. 
> 
> The moduli space $F_{{\mathrm{En}}, 2}$ embeds into the K3 moduli space $F_{(2,2,0)}$ via a canonical map $j$. Its image is the **Noether-Lefschetz locus** $\mathrm{NL}_{T_{\mathrm{En}}}$ defined by the primitive embedding of the anti-invariant lattice $T_{{\mathrm{En}}} = U(2) \oplus E_8(-2)$.
> 
> The Zariski closure $B := \overline{j(F_{{\mathrm{En}}, 2})} \subset \overline{F_{(2,2,0)}}$ parameterizes all KSBA limits of these double covers. The space fails to be normal along the boundary due to branching and boundary divisor intersections.
