> [!definition] ADE and BC Surfaces
> An **ADE surface** $(Y, C)$ is a log canonical log del Pezzo surface of index two where the dual intersection graph of the boundary $C$ is a simply-laced Dynkin diagram ($A_n, D_n, E_n$). These surfaces have canonical toric models determined by lattice polytopes in $\mathbf{Z}^2$ [@AT17].
> 
> **Priming**: A weighted blowup at a boundary node (corner blowup) that reduces the lattice length of a long edge.
> Allowed primings are restricted to preserve log canonicity and toricity.
> 
> **Folding**: Passing from a simply-laced ADE diagram to a non-simply-laced BC diagram by taking a quotient by an automorphism [@AT17; @AET23].
> - E.g., $A_{2n-1}$ folds to $C_n$.
> - This identifies symmetric nodes, leading to double bonds and merged nodes in the diagram.
> - Geometrically, the surface is replaced by a quotient $Y/\tau$.
>   Boundary components may become non-reduced, acquire multiplicities, or exhibit half-integral coefficients (half-divisors).
>   Passing to the canonical double cover branched over the half-integral loci restores the integral ADE boundary.
