> [!remark] Halphen surfaces and Coble surfaces
> A Halphen surface of index $n$ is a relatively minimal rational elliptic surface with fiber class $F$ satisfying $F \sim -nK_S$.
> Terminal Coble surfaces of K3 type are closely related to index-2 Halphen surfaces [@DZ99; @CDL24; @DK24].

## Blowdown relation

- Blowing down a $(-1)$-curve on a Coble surface produces a Halphen surface of index 2 [@CD12].

- The anti-bicanonical curve on the Coble surface is the proper transform of the unique singular point of an irreducible non-multiple fiber on the Halphen surface [@CD12].

- Conversely, blowing up the singular point of such a fiber on an index-2 Halphen surface produces a Coble surface [@CD12].

- In the classical $n=1$ construction, the image of the boundary curve is an irreducible plane sextic with ten $A_1$-singularities, and choosing a cubic through nine of them produces an index-2 Halphen pencil [@Cob19; @DK24].

## Moduli relevance

- In the classical $n=1$ case, the corresponding Halphen surfaces of index 2 form a 9-dimensional family [@DK24].

- This makes Halphen surfaces a geometric model for the same moduli count as $F_{\mathrm{Co}}$ and suggests a close correspondence between the two moduli problems [@DK24].
