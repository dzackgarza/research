> [!definition] The base surface $Y = \mathbf{P}^1 \times \mathbf{P}^1$
> Let $Y = \mathbf{P}^1 \times \mathbf{P}^1$ over $\mathbf{C}$.
> The Picard group $\operatorname{Pic}(Y) \cong \mathbf{Z}^2$ is freely generated by the pullbacks of hyperplanes $\ell_1, \ell_2$ from the two factors.
> The intersection numbers are $\ell_1^2 = \ell_2^2 = 0$ and $\ell_1 \cdot \ell_2 = 1$, yielding the lattice isometry: $$ (\operatorname{Pic}(Y), \cdot) \xrightarrow{\sim} \left( \mathbf{Z}^2, \begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix} \right) = U $$ The canonical divisor is $K_Y = -2\ell_1 - 2\ell_2$, giving a degree $K_Y^2 = 8$, making $Y$ a del Pezzo surface of degree 8.
