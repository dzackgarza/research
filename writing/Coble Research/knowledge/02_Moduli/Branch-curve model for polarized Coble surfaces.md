> [!construction] Branch-curve model for polarized Coble surfaces The polarized Coble locus is modeled on the Horikawa picture for $\tau$-invariant $(4,4)$-curves on $\mathbf{P}^1 \times \mathbf{P}^1$, but the Talks inbox refined the geometric slogan: passing through **exactly one** torus fixed point describes only the generic branch, not the whole closure.

## Established anchor

- The underlying Horikawa setup is recorded in [[Horikawa model for Coble surfaces]] [@Hor78; @AEGS23].
- In that model, a $\tau$-invariant anti-bicanonical curve through a fixed point yields a nodal K3 cover whose quotient is Coble.

## Generic versus closure behavior

- The generic polarized Coble branch is described by invariant curves with a nondegenerate quadratic part at a torus fixed point.
- The closure is expected to contain curves passing through several fixed points.
- The normalization should separate these branches by remembering a chosen Coble root rather than collapsing them into one undifferentiated boundary component.

## Symmetry heuristic

- The derivative report cluster repeatedly describes the acting symmetry as a $D_4 \rtimes (\mathbf{C}^*)^2$-type group.
- In that framework, a transitivity argument on the torus fixed points is one proposed route to irreducibility of the polarized Coble divisor.
- This symmetry statement is useful as a guide, but it still needs direct corroboration from an available primary source.
