> [!definition] Enriques surface
> An **Enriques surface** is a non-rational algebraic surface $Z$ of Kodaira dimension $\kappa(Z) = 0$ for which $h^1(\mathcal{O}_Z) = h^2(\mathcal{O}_Z) = 0$ and the canonical class $K_Z$ is nontrivial $2$-torsion in $\operatorname{Pic}(Z)$.
> 
> Equivalently, they are quotients $Z = X/\iota$ of [[K3 surface|K3 surfaces]] $X$ by fixed-point-free involutions $\iota$ (called Enriques involutions), satisfying $2K_Z \sim 0$ and $q(Z) = 0$.

## Properties

- They are among the first examples of non-rational surfaces with geometric genus $p_g = 0$.

- They are minimal surfaces.

- The fundamental group of the universal cover is $\pi_1(Z) \cong \mathbf{Z}_2$, generated by the covering involution, which acts freely and properly discontinuously on $X$.

- The Hodge diamond has $h^{1,1}(Z) = 10$, $h^{2,0}(Z) = h^{0,2}(Z) = 0$, confirming $p_g = 0$, and all other Hodge numbers vanish except $h^{0,0} = h^{2,2} = 1$.

- The free part of the cohomology is modeled by the [[Enriques lattice]].
