> [!remark] K3 covers of Coble surfaces Let $S$ be a terminal Coble surface of K3 type and write $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{O}_S(-K_S)$.
> A section of $\mathcal{L}^{\otimes 2}$ vanishing on $C \in |-2K_S|$ defines a branched double cover
> $$
> f \colon X \to S
> $$
> where $X$ is a K3 surface and the deck involution is nonsymplectic [@DK24; @CDL24].

## Fixed locus

- If $C = C_1 + \cdots + C_n$, then the fixed locus of the covering involution is the disjoint union of the preimages $f^{-1}(C_i)$.
- These are $(-2)$-curves on $X$ [@CD12; @CDL24].
- The invariant lattice is 2-elementary with invariants of the form [@CDL24]
  $$
  (r,a,\delta) = (10+n, 12-n, \delta).
  $$

## Important special cases

- $(10,10,0)$: empty fixed locus.
- $(10,8,0)$: fixed locus is two elliptic curves.
- Otherwise: fixed locus is one rational curve together with $n-1$ additional disjoint $(-2)$-curves [@CD12].

## Coble case with one boundary component

For the classical $n=1$ case, the fixed locus is one rational curve, and the invariant and anti-invariant lattices are the [[Coble lattice|Coble lattice data]] [@DM19; @CDL24]
$$
S_{\mathrm{Co}} = (11,11,1)_1, \qquad T_{\mathrm{Co}} = (11,11,1)_2.
$$
