> [!remark] Horikawa model for Coble surfaces Let $Y = \mathbf{P}^1 \times \mathbf{P}^1$ and let $\tau(x,y) = (-x,-y)$.
> If $B \in |-2K_Y|^\tau$ is a $\tau$-invariant anti-bicanonical curve passing through a $\tau$-fixed point, then the double cover of $Y$ branched along $B$ is a K3 surface with an $A_1$-singularity, and its quotient by the induced involution is a Coble surface [@Hor78; @AEGS23].

- If the branch curve avoids the $\tau$-fixed points, the same construction yields an Enriques surface instead [@Hor78; @AEGS23].
- This realizes the Coble locus as a boundary divisor inside Enriques moduli and gives a concrete geometric model for the transition from Enriques to Coble surfaces [@AEGS23].
- The corresponding toric surface and quotient geometry are part of the bridge between the period-domain and KSBA descriptions [@AEGS23].
- In the polarized Coble program, the inbox correction was that “passing through exactly one fixed point” should be read as a **generic branch description**, not as a statement about the full closure; see [[Branch-curve model for polarized Coble surfaces]].
