# Mathematical Lexicon

Each entry below is an admissible public noun. Definitions use earlier entries whenever
possible, so the lexicon grows by refinement rather than by ad hoc aliases.

## Basic sets and arithmetic

- `NaturalNumber` -- an element of `$\mathbb{N}$`.
- `Integer` -- an element of `$\mathbb{Z}$`.
- `Rational` -- an element of `$\mathbb{Q}$`.
- `RealNumber` -- an element of `$\mathbb{R}$`.
- `ComplexNumber` -- an element of `$\mathbb{C}$`.
- `Signature` -- an ordered pair `$(p,n) \in \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}$` recording
  the positive and negative indices of a nondegenerate real symmetric bilinear form.
- `Cardinality` -- the cardinality of a finite set, group, module, or quotient.
- `Rank` -- the rank of a finitely generated projective module, or the corresponding
  rank invariant of a lattice.

## Rings, ideals, and algebras

- `Ring` -- a ring `R` with additive identity and multiplicative identity.
- `CommutativeRing` -- a Ring with commutative multiplication.
- `IntegralDomain` -- a nonzero CommutativeRing with no zero divisors.
- `Field` -- a CommutativeRing in which every nonzero element is invertible.
- `PrincipalIdealDomain` -- an IntegralDomain in which every ideal is principal.
- `DedekindDomain` -- a noetherian, integrally closed IntegralDomain of Krull dimension
  one.
- `NumberField` -- a finite field extension of `$\mathbb{Q}$`.
- `PadicField` -- a finite extension of `$\mathbb{Q}_p$`.
- `RingElement` -- an element of a specified Ring.
- `Ideal` -- an ideal of a specified Ring.
- `QuotientRing` -- the ring `R/I` associated to a Ring `R` and an Ideal `I`. The ring
  `$\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$` is one instance, not the definition of this noun.
- `Algebra` -- a Ring equipped with a specified homomorphism from a base CommutativeRing.

## Groups and group actions

- `Group` -- a set with an associative binary operation, identity, and inverses.
- `AbelianGroup` -- a Group with commutative operation.
- `FiniteGroup` -- a Group with finite underlying set.
- `FiniteAbelianGroup` -- a finite AbelianGroup; equivalently, a finite
  `$\mathbb{Z}$`-module.
- `CyclicGroup` -- a Group generated by one element.
- `pElementaryGroup` -- an elementary abelian `p`-group, isomorphic to
  `$(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^r$` for some prime `p` and nonnegative integer `r`.
- `pPrimaryGroup` -- a finite AbelianGroup in which every element has order a power of
  the fixed prime `p`.
- `FreeGroup` -- the Group freely generated by a specified set.
- `MatrixGroup` -- a subgroup of `$\operatorname{GL}_n(R)$` for a specified Ring `R`.
- `PermutationGroup` -- a subgroup of the symmetric group of a specified set.
- `SymmetricGroup` -- the full permutation group of a specified finite set.
- `Subgroup` -- a subset of a Group that is itself a Group under the inherited operation.
- `NormalSubgroup` -- a Subgroup invariant under conjugation by the ambient Group.
- `QuotientGroup` -- the Group `G/N` for a Group `G` and a NormalSubgroup `N`.
- `GroupAction` -- a homomorphism from a Group to the permutation group of a set.
- `Homomorphism` -- a structure-preserving map in the named category.
- `Isomorphism` -- a bijective Homomorphism with a structure-preserving inverse.
- `Automorphism` -- an Isomorphism from an object to itself.
- `Endomorphism` -- a Homomorphism from an object to itself.
- `IsometryGroup` -- the Group of Isometries of a specified form or lattice.
- `FinitelyPresentedGroup` -- a quotient of a FreeGroup by the normal closure of a
  specified set of relators.
- `BraidGroup` -- the Group generated by braid generators subject to the Artin braid
  relations.
- `ArtinGroup` -- the Group associated to a Coxeter matrix by the corresponding braid
  relations.
- `RightAngledArtinGroup` -- the ArtinGroup whose defining Coxeter exponents are two or
  infinity.
- `AlternatingGroup` -- the subgroup of even permutations in a SymmetricGroup.
- `DihedralGroup` -- the Group of symmetries of a regular polygon.
- `KleinFourGroup` -- the AbelianGroup `$(\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z})^2$`.
- `QuaternionGroup` -- the nonabelian Group of order eight generated by `i,j,k` with
  `i^2=j^2=k^2=ijk=-1`.
- `TransitiveGroup` -- a PermutationGroup with one orbit on its domain.
- `PrimitiveGroup` -- a transitive PermutationGroup preserving no nontrivial partition
  of its domain.
- `GeneralLinearGroup` -- `$\operatorname{GL}(V)$`, the Group of invertible LinearMaps
  of a VectorSpace or FreeModule `V`.
- `SpecialLinearGroup` -- the subgroup of a GeneralLinearGroup with determinant one.
- `OrthogonalGroup` -- the Group of LinearMaps preserving a SymmetricBilinearForm.
- `SpecialOrthogonalGroup` -- the determinant-one subgroup of an OrthogonalGroup.
- `SymplecticGroup` -- the Group of LinearMaps preserving a nondegenerate alternating
  BilinearForm.
- `UnitaryGroup` -- the Group of LinearMaps preserving a HermitianForm.
- `SemidirectProduct` -- a Group formed from an action of one Group on another.

## Modules, vector spaces, and maps

- `RModule` -- a module over the Ring `R`.
- `FinitelyGeneratedModule` -- an RModule generated by finitely many elements.
- `ProjectiveModule` -- an RModule that is a direct summand of a FreeModule.
- `FinitelyGeneratedProjectiveModule` -- a ProjectiveModule that is finitely generated.
- `FreeModule` -- an RModule with a basis.
- `TorsionModule` -- an RModule whose elements are annihilated by nonzero elements of
  the base Ring in the relevant domain setting.
- `VectorSpace` -- an RModule whose base Ring is a Field.
- `ModuleElement` -- an element of a specified RModule.
- `Vector` -- an element of a specified VectorSpace or FreeModule.
- `Submodule` -- a subset of an RModule closed under addition and scalar multiplication.
- `QuotientModule` -- the module `M/N` for an RModule `M` and a Submodule `N`.
- `HomModule` -- the RModule of R-linear maps between two specified RModules.
- `EndomorphismRing` -- the Ring of endomorphisms of a specified RModule.
- `LinearMap` -- an R-linear Homomorphism of RModules.
- `SemilinearMap` -- an additive map `f:M\to N` satisfying
  `f(rm)=\sigma(r)f(m)` for a specified Ring homomorphism `\sigma`.
- `ModuleWithBasis` -- a FreeModule with a distinguished basis whose labels carry
  mathematical meaning.
- `Representation` -- a Homomorphism from a Group or Algebra to the endomorphisms of a
  specified Module.
- `InvariantModule` -- the Submodule of a Representation fixed by a specified action.
- `FilteredModule` -- an RModule with an indexed family of Submodules compatible with
  its structure maps.
- `FilteredVectorSpace` -- a VectorSpace with an indexed filtration by Subspaces.
- `GradedModule` -- an RModule decomposed as a direct sum `$\bigoplus_d M_d$` indexed
  by a grading set.
- `GradedAlgebra` -- an Algebra whose multiplication respects a direct-sum grading.
- `FinitelyPresentedModule` -- the cokernel of a map between finite FreeModules.
- `FinitelyPresentedGradedModule` -- a GradedModule with a finite graded presentation.
- `FreeResolution` -- an exact complex of FreeModules resolving a specified Module.
- `SyzygyModule` -- a Kernel in a presentation or FreeResolution.
- `Matrix` -- a rectangular array of elements of a specified Ring, representing a
  LinearMap or a form after bases have been fixed.
- `BilinearForm` -- a bilinear map `b : M \times M \to R` on an RModule `M`.
- `SymmetricBilinearForm` -- a BilinearForm satisfying `b(x,y)=b(y,x)`.
- `QuadraticForm` -- a quadratic map on an RModule together with its associated
  BilinearForm where the coefficient regime permits it.
- `GramMatrix` -- the Matrix `$(b(e_i,e_j))$` of a BilinearForm in a specified basis.

## Lattices

- `RLattice` -- a pair `$(L,b)$` with `L` a finitely generated projective RModule and
  `b : L \times L \to R` a SymmetricBilinearForm.
- `Lattice` -- an RLattice with `R := \mathbb{Z}`.
- `LatticeElement` -- an element of the RModule underlying an RLattice.
- `NondegenerateRLattice` -- an RLattice for which the adjoint map
  `L \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(L,R)` induced by `b` is injective.
- `DegenerateRLattice` -- an RLattice with nonzero radical
  `$\operatorname{rad}(L)=\{x\in L : b(x,y)=0\text{ for all }y\in L\}$`.
- `NondegenerateLattice` -- a Lattice that is a NondegenerateRLattice.
- `DegenerateLattice` -- a Lattice that is a DegenerateRLattice.
- `UnimodularLattice` -- a NondegenerateLattice for which the adjoint map to the dual is
  an isomorphism.
- `EvenLattice` -- a Lattice satisfying `b(x,x) \in 2\mathbb{Z}` for every `x \in L`.
- `IntegralLattice` -- not a separate noun: every Lattice is integral because its form
  is `$\mathbb{Z}$`-valued by definition.
- `DefiniteLattice` -- a Lattice whose real extension is positive definite or negative
  definite.
- `PositiveDefiniteLattice` -- a Lattice whose real extension has
  `b(x,x)>0` for every nonzero vector.
- `NegativeDefiniteLattice` -- a Lattice whose real extension has
  `b(x,x)<0` for every nonzero vector.
- `IndefiniteLattice` -- a Lattice whose real extension has both positive and negative
  directions.
- `HyperbolicPlane` -- the rank-two Lattice with Gram matrix
  `$\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}$`.
- `HyperbolicLattice` -- an orthogonal direct sum of HyperbolicPlanes.
- `DualLattice` -- for a NondegenerateLattice `L`, the lattice
  `L^\vee=\{x\in L\otimes\mathbb{Q}:b(x,L)\subseteq\mathbb{Z}\}`.
- `LatticeMorphism` -- an R-linear map between the underlying RModules of two
  RLattices.
- `Isometry` -- a LatticeMorphism `f` satisfying `b(fx,fy)=b(x,y)`.
- `Similitude` -- a LatticeMorphism `f` satisfying
  `b(fx,fy)=\lambda b(x,y)` for a specified scalar `\lambda`.
- `Genus` -- the local isometry class of a Lattice at every prime and at the real place.
- `EuclideanLattice` -- a discrete subgroup of a finite-dimensional EuclideanSpace that
  spans its real vector space.
- `ToricLattice` -- a finitely generated free AbelianGroup used as the lattice of
  one-parameter subgroups or characters of a torus.
- `CharacterLattice` -- the ToricLattice `M=\operatorname{Hom}(T,\mathbb{G}_m)` of
  characters of an algebraic torus `T`.
- `CocharacterLattice` -- the ToricLattice
  `N=\operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb{G}_m,T)` of one-parameter subgroups of `T`.
- `Overlattice` -- a Lattice `L'` containing a Lattice `L` with finite quotient
  `L'/L`.
- `Sublattice` -- a finite-rank submodule of a Lattice equipped with the restricted
  BilinearForm.
- `PrimitiveSublattice` -- a Sublattice `S\subseteq L` for which `L/S` is torsion-free.
- `OrthogonalComplement` -- the Submodule
  `S^\perp=\{x\in L:b(x,s)=0\text{ for all }s\in S\}` of a SymmetricBilinearForm
  module.
- `RootLattice` -- the Lattice generated by the roots of a RootSystem.
- `CorootLattice` -- the Lattice generated by the coroots of a RootSystem.
- `WeightLattice` -- the lattice dual to the CorootLattice under the natural pairing.

## Discriminant bilinear and quadratic forms

- `DiscriminantBilinearForm` -- for a NondegenerateLattice `L`, the ordered pair
  `$(A_L,b_L)$`, where `A_L=L^\vee/L` is a FiniteAbelianGroup and
  `b_L:A_L\times A_L\to\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}` is the induced bilinear form.
- `DiscriminantQuadraticForm` -- for an EvenLattice `L`, the ordered pair
  `$(A_L,q_L)$`, where `A_L=L^\vee/L` and
  `q_L:A_L\to\mathbb{Q}/2\mathbb{Z}` is the induced quadratic form.
- `DiscriminantForm` -- not an admissible noun, because it does not determine whether
  the structure is bilinear or quadratic.
- `discriminant_group()` -- the construction on a Lattice that returns the appropriate
  DiscriminantBilinearForm or DiscriminantQuadraticForm. Its first constituent is a
  FiniteAbelianGroup; that constituent does not acquire a separate
  `DiscriminantGroup` type.

## Geometry, arrangements, and combinatorics

- `EuclideanSpace` -- the affine metric space `$\mathbb{E}^n$`.
- `SphericalSpace` -- the unit sphere `$\mathbb{S}^n$` with its spherical geometry.
- `HyperbolicSpace` -- the constant-negative-curvature space `$\mathbb{H}^n$`.
- `Hyperplane` -- a codimension-one affine, spherical, or hyperbolic subspace in the
  stated ambient geometry.
- `HalfSpace` -- one of the closed sides determined by a Hyperplane.
- `Polyhedron` -- an intersection of finitely many HalfSpaces in a stated ambient
  geometry.
- `Polytope` -- a bounded Polyhedron; thus an intersection of finitely many HalfSpaces
  determined by a configuration of Hyperplanes in `$\mathbb{E}^n$,
  $\mathbb{S}^n$, or $\mathbb{H}^n$`, subject to the appropriate boundedness or
  properness condition.
- `Polygon` -- a two-dimensional Polytope.
- `Simplex` -- the convex hull of an affinely independent finite set of points.
- `Cone` -- a subset of a real VectorSpace closed under multiplication by nonnegative
  real scalars.
- `Fan` -- a finite collection of polyhedral Cones closed under faces, such that the
  intersection of two Cones is a face of each.
- `HyperplaneArrangement` -- a finite collection of Hyperplanes in a common ambient
  geometry.
- `Chamber` -- a connected component of the complement of a HyperplaneArrangement.
- `Graph` -- a set of vertices together with a set of edges incident to pairs of vertices.

## Posets and toric geometry

- `Poset` -- a set equipped with a reflexive, antisymmetric, transitive relation.
- `MeetSemilattice` -- a Poset in which every pair of elements has a greatest lower
  bound.
- `JoinSemilattice` -- a Poset in which every pair of elements has a least upper bound.
- `LatticePoset` -- a Poset that is both a MeetSemilattice and a JoinSemilattice.
- `FiniteLatticePoset` -- a finite LatticePoset.
- `Atom` -- an element covering the bottom element of a LatticePoset.
- `Coatom` -- an element covered by the top element of a LatticePoset.
- `ToricCone` -- a rational polyhedral Cone in a ToricLattice tensored with
  `$\mathbb{R}$`.
- `ToricFan` -- a Fan of ToricCones.
- `FanMorphism` -- a homomorphism of ToricLattices mapping every Cone of one ToricFan
  into a Cone of another.
- `LatticePolytope` -- a Polytope whose vertices lie in a ToricLattice.

The noun `LatticePoset` belongs to order theory and `ToricLattice` belongs to toric
geometry. Neither is an RLattice or a Lattice unless the additional bilinear-form data
has been specified.

## Local forms and arithmetic invariants

- `QuadraticSpace` -- a VectorSpace over a Field equipped with a QuadraticForm.
- `HermitianForm` -- a sesquilinear form `h:V\times V\to K` satisfying
  `h(y,x)=\overline{h(x,y)}` for a specified involution of `K`.
- `AlternatingBilinearForm` -- a BilinearForm satisfying `b(x,x)=0` for every `x`.
- `IsotropicVector` -- a nonzero vector `x` with `q(x)=0` for a QuadraticForm `q`.
- `AnisotropicQuadraticForm` -- a QuadraticForm with no IsotropicVector.
- `HasseInvariant` -- the local invariant of a nondegenerate QuadraticForm over a local
  field that participates in its classification.
- `WittIndex` -- the dimension of a maximal totally isotropic subspace of a
  QuadraticSpace.
- `JordanDecomposition` -- the orthogonal decomposition of a local quadratic lattice
  into scaled unimodular constituents.
- `LocalGenus` -- the isometry class of a Lattice after extension to a local ring or
  field.
- `LocalGenusSymbol` -- the symbolic datum encoding a LocalGenus.
- `ThetaSeries` -- the generating series whose coefficient at `q^m` counts vectors of
  norm or quadratic value `m`.
- `RepresentationNumber` -- the number of vectors represented by a specified
  QuadraticForm at a specified value.
- `LocalDensity` -- the local factor measuring representations of a form over a local
  field.
- `BrownInvariant` -- the invariant of a finite quadratic form defined through its
  normalized Gauss sum.

## Commutative algebra

- `CommutativeAlgebra` -- an Algebra whose multiplication is commutative.
- `PolynomialRing` -- the ring of polynomials over a specified coefficient Ring in a
  specified set of variables.
- `PowerSeriesRing` -- the ring of formal power series over a specified coefficient
  Ring.
- `FractionField` -- the field of fractions of an IntegralDomain.
- `Localization` -- the Ring `S^{-1}R` obtained by inverting a multiplicatively closed
  subset `S` of a CommutativeRing `R`.
- `PrimeIdeal` -- a proper Ideal `\mathfrak p` for which `R/\mathfrak p` is an
  IntegralDomain.
- `MaximalIdeal` -- a proper Ideal `\mathfrak m` for which `R/\mathfrak m` is a Field.
- `PrimaryIdeal` -- an Ideal `Q` such that `ab\in Q` and `a\notin Q` imply
  `b^n\in Q` for some positive integer `n`.
- `RadicalIdeal` -- an Ideal `I` satisfying `I=\sqrt I`.
- `AssociatedPrime` -- a PrimeIdeal that is the annihilator of an element of a specified
  Module.
- `PrimaryDecomposition` -- an expression of an Ideal as an intersection of
  PrimaryIdeals.
- `GroebnerBasis` -- a generating set of an Ideal whose leading terms generate its
  initial ideal for a specified monomial order.
- `HilbertFunction` -- the function recording the dimensions of the graded pieces of a
  GradedModule.
- `HilbertPolynomial` -- the polynomial eventually equal to the HilbertFunction.
- `KrullDimension` -- the supremal length of chains of PrimeIdeals.
- `Height` -- the supremal length of chains of PrimeIdeals ending at a given PrimeIdeal.
- `Codimension` -- the difference between ambient dimension and subobject dimension in
  a specified equidimensional setting.

## Algebraic geometry

- `AffineScheme` -- the Scheme `\operatorname{Spec}(R)` of a CommutativeRing `R`.
- `ProjectiveScheme` -- the Scheme `\operatorname{Proj}(S)` of a graded ring `S`.
- `Scheme` -- a locally ringed space locally isomorphic to an AffineScheme.
- `Variety` -- a reduced irreducible Scheme of finite type over a specified field.
- `Subscheme` -- a Scheme equipped with an immersion into an ambient Scheme.
- `ClosedSubscheme` -- a Subscheme defined locally by a sheaf of ideals.
- `ClosedEmbedding` -- an isomorphism onto a ClosedSubscheme.
- `SchemeMorphism` -- a morphism of locally ringed spaces between Schemes.
- `Curve` -- a Scheme of dimension one in the declared geometric setting.
- `Surface` -- a Scheme of dimension two in the declared geometric setting.
- `Hypersurface` -- a ClosedSubscheme locally defined by one equation.
- `CompleteIntersection` -- a Subscheme locally defined by a regular sequence of the
  expected codimension.
- `TangentSpace` -- the Zariski tangent VectorSpace at a specified point of a Scheme.
- `SingularLocus` -- the ClosedSubscheme where the local rings are not regular.
- `Divisor` -- a formal integer combination of codimension-one subvarieties.
- `WeilDivisor` -- a Divisor on a normal integral Scheme.
- `CartierDivisor` -- a Divisor locally defined by one nonzero rational function.
- `LineBundle` -- an invertible sheaf, equivalently a locally free sheaf of rank one.
- `PicardGroup` -- the Group of isomorphism classes of LineBundles under tensor
  product.
- `Jacobian` -- the abelian variety representing degree-zero divisor classes of a smooth
  projective curve.
- `EllipticCurve` -- a smooth projective genus-one Curve with a specified rational point.
- `HyperellipticCurve` -- a smooth projective Curve admitting a degree-two map to the
  projective line.
- `ToricVariety` -- the normal variety associated to a ToricFan.
- `ToricDivisor` -- a Divisor invariant under the dense algebraic-torus action on a
  ToricVariety.
- `ChowGroup` -- the group of algebraic cycles modulo rational equivalence.
- `CohomologyRing` -- a graded ring formed from cohomology groups with cup product.
- `MoriCone` -- the cone of effective curve classes of a projective variety.
- `KahlerCone` -- the cone of Kahler classes in the real cohomology of a compact
  Kahler manifold.

## Tensor, homological, and differential structures

- `AssociativeAlgebra` -- an RModule with an associative R-bilinear multiplication.
- `FiniteDimensionalAlgebra` -- an AssociativeAlgebra finite-dimensional over its base
  Field.
- `TensorProduct` -- the RModule `M\otimes_R N` universal for R-bilinear maps from
  `M\times N`.
- `TensorAlgebra` -- the Algebra `T(M)=\bigoplus_{n\geq0}M^{\otimes n}`.
- `SymmetricAlgebra` -- the quotient of a TensorAlgebra imposing commutativity.
- `ExteriorAlgebra` -- the quotient of a TensorAlgebra imposing `x\otimes x=0`.
- `CliffordAlgebra` -- the quotient of a TensorAlgebra determined by a QuadraticForm.
- `ChainComplex` -- a sequence of Modules and differentials `d_n:C_n\to C_{n-1}` with
  `d_{n-1}d_n=0`.
- `CochainComplex` -- a sequence of Modules and differentials `d^n:C^n\to C^{n+1}` with
  `d^{n+1}d^n=0`.
- `ChainMap` -- a degree-preserving family of maps commuting with differentials.
- `ChainHomotopy` -- a family of maps witnessing that two ChainMaps differ by a boundary.
- `HomologyGroup` -- `H_n(C)=\ker d_n/\operatorname{im}d_{n+1}` of a ChainComplex.
- `CohomologyGroup` -- `H^n(C)=\ker d^n/\operatorname{im}d^{n-1}` of a CochainComplex.
- `FreeResolution` -- an exact augmented ChainComplex of FreeModules resolving a
  Module.
- `KoszulComplex` -- the ChainComplex associated to a sequence of elements of a Ring.
- `HochschildComplex` -- the ChainComplex computing Hochschild homology of an Algebra.
- `DifferentiableManifold` -- a Hausdorff second-countable manifold with a smooth atlas.
- `VectorField` -- a smooth section of the tangent bundle of a DifferentiableManifold.
- `TensorField` -- a smooth section of a tensor bundle.
- `DifferentialForm` -- an alternating covariant TensorField.
- `DeRhamComplex` -- the CochainComplex of DifferentialForms with exterior derivative.
- `DeRhamCohomology` -- the CohomologyRing of the DeRhamComplex.
- `HodgeDual` -- the metric-dependent isomorphism between differential forms of
  complementary degree.

## Valuations and local fields

- `Valuation` -- a map measuring multiplicative order, satisfying
  `v(xy)=v(x)+v(y)` and `v(x+y)\geq\min(v(x),v(y))`.
- `pAdicValuation` -- the Valuation `v_p` normalized by `v_p(p)=1`.
- `PadicIntegerRing` -- the valuation ring `\mathbb{Z}_p` of the p-adic field.
- `PadicField` -- the completion `\mathbb{Q}_p` of `\mathbb{Q}` for the p-adic
  Valuation.
- `Uniformizer` -- an element of valuation one in a discrete valuation ring.
- `ResidueField` -- the quotient of a local ring by its maximal ideal.
- `Completion` -- a complete topological ring or module obtained from a specified
  topology or filtration.

## Lie algebras and representations

- `LieAlgebra` -- an RModule with an R-bilinear bracket satisfying antisymmetry and the
  Jacobi identity.
- `LieSubalgebra` -- a Submodule closed under the Lie bracket.
- `LieIdeal` -- a LieSubalgebra `I` satisfying `[L,I]\subseteq I`.
- `QuotientLieAlgebra` -- the LieAlgebra `L/I` for a LieIdeal `I`.
- `UniversalEnvelopingAlgebra` -- the AssociativeAlgebra universal for LieAlgebra maps
  from a LieAlgebra into the commutator LieAlgebra of an AssociativeAlgebra.
- `PBWBasis` -- the ordered monomial basis supplied by the Poincare--Birkhoff--Witt
  theorem.
- `LieAlgebraRepresentation` -- a LieAlgebra Homomorphism into an EndomorphismRing.
- `VermaModule` -- the highest-weight module induced from a Borel subalgebra and a
  weight.
- `CartanSubalgebra` -- a nilpotent self-normalizing LieSubalgebra in the usual finite
  dimensional setting.
- `RootSpace` -- an eigenspace of a CartanSubalgebra acting on a LieAlgebra.

## Roots and reflection groups

- `CartanType` -- the finite or affine type encoded by a CartanMatrix or DynkinDiagram.
- `DynkinDiagram` -- the labeled graph encoding the simple roots and Cartan integers of
  a root system.
- `CartanMatrix` -- the integer matrix `A=(a_{ij})` with
  `a_{ij}=\langle\alpha_i,\alpha_j^\vee\rangle` for simple roots and coroots.
- `CoxeterMatrix` -- the matrix `M=(m_{ij})` specifying the orders of products of
  distinguished Coxeter generators.
- `Root` -- a vector of prescribed norm in a Lattice, usually norm two in an
  EvenLattice.
- `Coroot` -- the vector or covector associated to a nonzero root by its reflection
  pairing.
- `Weight` -- an element of the WeightLattice.
- `FundamentalWeight` -- the Weight paired with one simple Coroot as one and every
  other simple Coroot as zero.
- `RootSystem` -- a finite set of Roots closed under the reflections determined by those
  Roots and satisfying the usual integrality conditions.
- `Reflection` -- the isometry in a nonisotropic vector `r` given by
  `x \mapsto x - 2b(x,r)b(r,r)^{-1}r`.
- `WeylGroup` -- the Group generated by the Reflections in a RootSystem.
- `CoxeterGroup` -- a Group with a Coxeter presentation.
- `AffineWeylGroup` -- the CoxeterGroup generated by affine reflections associated to
  an affine root system.
- `ExtendedAffineWeylGroup` -- the semidirect extension of an AffineWeylGroup by the
  relevant coweight-lattice quotient.
- `BruhatOrder` -- the partial order on a CoxeterGroup determined by containment of
  reduced subwords.
- `WeakOrder` -- the partial order on a CoxeterGroup determined by length-additive
  multiplication by simple reflections.

## Standard constructions and return contracts

- `base_ring()` -- returns the Ring of scalars of an RModule or RLattice.
- `signature()` -- returns `tuple[NaturalNumber, NaturalNumber]`, the direct
  representation of a Signature.
- `gram_matrix()` -- returns the GramMatrix in the chosen basis.
- `dual()` -- returns the DualLattice.
- `radical()` -- returns the radical Submodule of a DegenerateRLattice.
- `isometry_group()` -- returns the IsometryGroup.
- `genus()` -- returns the Genus.
- `from_gram_matrix()` -- constructs an RLattice from a GramMatrix and declared base
  Ring.
- `from_generators()` -- constructs the object generated by the declared generators in
  the relevant category.
- `parse_*` and `validate_*` -- operations on representations that either produce a
  named object or reject the representation; they are not type names.

## Contributing

The glossary is mathematical doctrine, not a typing inventory. Add a term within its
mathematical category as a list entry with a definition built from existing entries.
Create a new category only for a genuinely distinct mathematical branch.

### Let mathematics decide the noun

Use the relevant mathematical literature as the oracle. A candidate name is admissible
only when its instances, ambient set or category, and defining maps, axioms, or
invariants can be stated in ordinary mathematical language. Software roles, validation
history, provenance, storage, and interoperability boundaries do not meet this test.

### Name entities, not type-checker conveniences

A rule to type every program value is not a rule to invent a noun for every program
value. The name must denote an object, class of objects, element, morphism,
construction, or established invariant. Direct products and ambient objects remain
direct products and ambient objects.

The standard example is `signature()`. The method already names the invariant, and its
value is the ordinary pair `$(p,n)\in\mathbb{N}\times\mathbb{N}$`. A nominal
`SignaturePair` type adds neither mathematical structure nor useful static separation;
it makes the reader look up a fact the return type should state directly.

### Preserve the ambient category

Apply inclusions and declared meanings recursively. Since
`$\mathbb{Z}\subseteq\mathbb{Q}$`, a value allowed to be integral or rational is a
`Rational`, not `Integer | Rational`. A scalar is an element of a stated base Ring or
Field, not a free-standing `ExactScalar` category.

The canonical terms on this page bind every later definition and annotation. Here
`Integer` means an element of `$\mathbb{Z}$` and raw `int` is not part of the public
vocabulary. Thus `tuple[int, int]` is internally inconsistent even before its separate
mathematical error: signature coordinates are NaturalNumbers, so the contract is
`tuple[NaturalNumber, NaturalNumber]`.

### Keep defined structures intact

Name a tuple, quotient, or morphism by the complete object it defines. A
`QuotientRing` is `R/I`, not a synonym for one instance
`$\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$. A DiscriminantBilinearForm is the pair $(G,b)$ and a
DiscriminantQuadraticForm is the pair $(G,q)$`. Their first constituents are
FiniteAbelianGroups; do not promote them to a separate `DiscriminantGroup` type.

### Keep representations and operations out of the ontology

Matrices, coordinates, and parsed inputs may represent mathematical objects, but they do
not create new kinds merely by doing so. `Codec`, `Raw*`, `Seam`, provenance prefixes,
and implementation-class aliases describe program arrangements rather than mathematical
entities. Put such facts in constructors, parsers, adapters, or documentation outside
the glossary.

### Check internal coherence before adding a term

- State the mathematical definition using existing entries whenever possible.
- Check that every referenced type uses the canonical vocabulary already established on
  this page.
- Prefer a standard inclusion, product, quotient, or structured object to a new wrapper.
- Check that the proposed term has established mathematical usage.
- Put the entry in the category where a reader will look for its mathematical relatives.
